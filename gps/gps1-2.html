Pairing is Caring

Taniya Prabhakar

What is Pair Programming?

Pair programming is when two people work together on a project or some type of coding exercise at one computer. During this collaboration, one person takes on the role of a Driver, and the other, a Navigator. In this process, both sides learn to acknowledge and truly listen to each other as they bounce ideas back and forth. The Navigator guides the Driver as she/he codes, and provides suggestions on different ways to find a solution.

Why is Pair Programming Important to DBC?

Pair Programming is important to DBC because it cultivates an essential habit of working with others and communicating effectively. This is hugely important in the workplace on a daily basis since developers continuously work with other developers. Knowing how to interact with others via patience, and understanding your own thought process will help you become a better collaborator.

Comparing Driver and Navigator...

The Driver and Navigator work together by throwing out suggestions and ways to design and execute the code, but the Driver is in control of the keyboard while the Navigator gives information for what they should be seeing as output. The Driver is a little more hands-on (the keyboard), but the Navigator has just as important of a role as they over-see the output and suggest different potential solutions.
